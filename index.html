Abstract

Ultrasound is a primary diagnostic tool commonly used to evaluate internal body structures, including organs, blood vessels, the musculoskeletal system, and fetal development. However, diagnosing abnormalities in ultrasound images is challenging for less - experienced clinicians due to issues like operator dependence, noise, limited field of view, difficulties in imaging through bone and air, and system - to - system variability.

The development of artificial intelligence (AI) technology has the potential to assist in ultrasound image diagnosis. Nevertheless, many existing databases are created using a single device type and collection site, which restricts the generalizability of machine learning models.

To address this, we have collected a large, publicly accessible ultrasound challenge database. This database is sourced from publicly available Internet data and contains a total of 1,833 unique ultrasound data entries. It encompasses 13 different types of ultrasound image anomalies, and all data have been anonymized. Our goal is to significantly enhance the performance of AI - assisted ultrasound diagnosis. Additionally, our data - sharing program aims to support benchmark testing of ultrasound disease diagnosis in multi - center environments.

Highlights

  • First to use public media to obtain valuable medical video data.
  • Build a large and well - annotated ultrasound video diagnostic dataset.
  • Introduce a new benchmark for ultrasound video classification.
